Egg White Custard Cream

Instractions:

  • 150g egg white
  • 400g sugar
  • 1/3 tsp citric acid (1-2g)
  • 70g of water

Instractions:

  1. Combine sugar, citric acid, water in a saucepan and put on fire.
  2. Stuff egg whites into a clean bowl. If the bowl was used for oil creams, then wash twice and, to be sure, wipe with vodka (and wash again).
  3. When the syrup is cooked to 120 degrees, we begin to beat the whites at high power until dense peaks.
  4. When the syrup reaches 130 degrees, we begin to pour the syrup in a THIN stream into the whipping proteins. For me, 10 degrees (from 120 to 130) is just enough for perfect whipping of proteins until the syrup is ready. It may take you more or less time. For the first time, you can do this: first boil the syrup to 130, then start whipping the whites. When the whites are whipped, quickly heat the syrup to 130 degrees and pour in.
  5. The most important thing: when you pour in the syrup, beat for another 15 seconds (just count) and turn off the mixer. This condition is relevant for good, powerful, stationary mixers. If the mixer is manual or weak stationary, then continue to beat until "caves" appear. Because until the first signs of thickening of the cream. Everything, the cream is ready and wants to become beautiful flowers.
